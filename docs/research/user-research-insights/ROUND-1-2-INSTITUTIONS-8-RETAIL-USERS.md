The following insights represent perspectives from liquidity suppliers, sophisticated users, and crypto-native retail participants.

---

## Cross-Interview Insights

1. **Liquidity is the most critical factor**
   - Every serious participant makes their decisions based on liquidity, volume, and orderbook depth.
   - Incentives help *bootstrap*, but cannot substitute for real liquidity.
   - Programmatic capital only enters once a base liquidity threshold exists ("low 7 figures goes a long way" early; much higher later).
   - Users will tolerate bad UX if liquidity or exclusive assets exist.

2. **Programmatic does not mean AI-Native**
   - All MMs are deeply programmatic, but explicitly not AI-agent driven.
   - APIs and SDKs matter enormously, but as deterministic, transparent infrastructure, not agent orchestration layers.
   - Universal complaint: APIs and SDKs are bad everywhere, including Hyperliquid.
   - Pain points:
     - Poor documentation
     - Inefficient SDKs
     - Lack of transparency around liquidation & risk mechanics

3. **Asset Novelty beats Feature Novelty**
   - Net-new or exclusive markets repeatedly surfaced as the strongest pull. Sophisticated actors will handle worse experiences to access new assets.
   - Retail participants split on this:
     - Long-term holders prefer UX
     - Degen traders want fast access to new assets, regardless of UX
   - Equity perps viewed as the largest long-term prize, but with major compliance and hedging constraints (for institutions).
   - Prediction markets: high interest, insufficient liquidity today.

4. **Vaults are viewed with skepticism**
   - Strong warning against Nexus running active vault strategies without HFT-grade expertise.

---

## Net-New Insights

- DEXs are increasingly used as hedging venues, not primary execution, for some sophisticated players.
- Gas costs for order placement/cancellation are a hidden but critical MM retention factor.
- Word-of-mouth and "unforgettable moments" (e.g., instant execution UX) matter for retail users.
- Clear segmentation emerged:
  - **MMs:** liquidity, rebates, DevX, risk clarity
  - **Power users:** depth, simplicity, no vault risk
  - **Researchers/influencers:** novelty, explainability, clean UX
- Strong belief that equity perps will be massive across the board.
- Frontend providing additional market information (e.g. social layer, Bloomberg-style awareness) beyond just the platform and crypto assets is a way to engage retail users daily.
- Users are willing to pay for premium order placement on low-liquidity markets.
- Providing users exposure to new markets that they can't access otherwise is a strong pull factor (e.g. Hong Kong IPO market directly called out for US investors).
- A points program is not enough.
- Mobile experience and seamless UX are a big draw for retail users.
- Perpetuals are suited for 'degen'-type users, even among crypto-natives. They don't make sense for long-term holders of spot. Leaning into user groups that trade or gamble frequently will be highest value.
- Creating arenas for users to have their own edge is attractive to retail users.
